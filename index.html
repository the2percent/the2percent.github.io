06 — The System

What it does and why

High-level architecture. The formula stays private. The thinking is here.

Core Mechanism
The system uses an adaptive Supertrend indicator — a volatility-normalised trend filter that adjusts its sensitivity to market conditions dynamically. Unlike a fixed-parameter trend follower, it recalibrates how aggressively it tracks price based on recent volatility. This allows it to stay in trending moves longer while tightening during choppy regimes.
Stoploss Logic
A layered stoploss structure sits on top of the trend signal. It is not a fixed percentage — it is a function of recent price behaviour. The result is that position exits on losing trades happen faster in high-volatility environments, limiting the worst daily losses to under ₹15K even when markets moved aggressively.
Instrument Selection
Dixon Technologies and BSE Ltd were selected for their trending behaviour, liquidity, and distinct sector characteristics — one a capital goods story, one a financial infrastructure play. A 50/50 capital split across instruments reduces single-stock concentration without introducing correlation drag.

07 — What This Is Not

Read this before drawing conclusions

The numbers are honest. So are the limitations.

Honest caveats
  • This is a backtest, not a live trading record. Past performance in simulated conditions does not guarantee future results in live markets.
  • Slippage is not modelled. In a real intraday environment, entry and exit prices may differ from the signal price — particularly in volatile periods. Fees are fully accounted for, but market impact is not.
  • Dixon Technologies and BSE Ltd exhibited strong trending behaviour over this period. A different instrument set or a mean-reverting market regime may produce materially different results.
  • The 5× leverage used here amplifies both gains and losses. Capital adequacy calculations show a floor of ₹1,03,656 — but margin calls in live markets can happen faster than historical data suggests.
  • This is not SEBI registered investment advice. The system is published to document a methodology and showcase algorithmic thinking — not to solicit capital or clients.
