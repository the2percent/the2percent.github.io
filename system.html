The System

What it does

A systematic intraday trend-following approach on NSE equities. This page describes behaviour and design intent — not the proprietary signal logic behind it.

Core approach
The system identifies and follows directional price moves during the trading session. It reacts to market conditions rather than forecasting them — entering when a trend is confirmed and exiting when the move loses momentum or reverses.
Risk management
Losing trades are closed systematically based on recent price behaviour, not fixed percentage stops. The design prioritises limiting tail losses during volatile sessions while allowing winning trades to develop.
Adaptive behaviour
The system adjusts its sensitivity to current market conditions. In trending environments it holds positions longer. In choppy, low-conviction regimes it tightens exposure. This is built into the design — not a manual override.
Portfolio construction
Capital is deployed across a concentrated intraday portfolio on NSE cash equities. Positions are selected for trending behaviour and liquidity. Allocation is structured to limit single-position concentration without diluting the edge.

System class

This system belongs to the trend-following family — a well-documented class of systematic strategies that profits from persistent directional moves rather than mean reversion. The edge does not come from predicting where price will go. It comes from a structural asymmetry: let winning trades run, cut losing trades quickly. Over enough occurrences, a win rate above 50% combined with winners larger than losers compounds into the return profile shown on the Performance page.
